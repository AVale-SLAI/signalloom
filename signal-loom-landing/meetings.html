The problem

Meeting recordings are black holes with timestamps on the lid.

You record everything. You search nothing. Your AI answers from meeting data are ungrounded because there's no structure to ground them in.

Failure mode 1

The chunk problem

Whisper outputs continuous text. To make it searchable, you chunk it — but chunks have no temporal anchor. A search result says "pricing was discussed" without saying when, in which meeting, or by whom. Your RAG pipeline returns text fragments, not evidence.

Failure mode 2

The hallucination amplifier

AI systems trained on unchunked transcript text have no citation path. They can't verify whether an answer came from the Q3 planning meeting or the offsite. Every ungrounded answer from meeting data is a hallucination risk — and you won't catch it until a customer does.

Failure mode 3

The post-processing sink

Every team building meeting intelligence builds the same pipeline: Whisper → chunking → speaker diarization → timestamp alignment → entity extraction → schema normalization. That's 6–12 weeks before you ship one useful feature. Weeks you didn't budget.

Failure mode 4

The "which meeting?" problem

Multiple meetings. Same attendees. Same topics. Without provenance metadata, a retrieval system can't distinguish the Q1 planning discussion from the Q3 retrospective. You get answers from the wrong context — and no signal that anything went wrong.

Failure mode 5

The schema drift trap

Whisper output format changes between model versions. Your parsing logic breaks. Your RAG pipeline silently degrades. You're now maintaining a model output contract for a product you don't own — and it's not your core competency.

Failure mode 6

The "it's indexed" illusion

You ran Whisper and loaded it into your vector DB. You're not searching the meeting — you're searching a text representation of the meeting. Temporal context, speaker identity, and provenance are all gone. What comes back is text. Not knowledge.

The gap

What teams build vs. what they need.

The instinct is right: record everything, index it, build AI on top. The execution fails at the data layer.

What teams build

  • Whisper API call → plain text
  • Split by sentence or fixed token count
  • Load into vector DB as text chunks
  • Semantic search over embeddings
  • LLM answers from top-k chunks
  • No timestamps, no speaker labels, no provenance
  • No verification path for AI answers
  • Weeks of custom pipeline work

What they actually need

  • One call → timestamped, speaker-labeled segments
  • Each segment is a citable artifact
  • Provenance metadata: source URL, meeting context
  • RAG retrieval returns temporal windows, not text fragments
  • AI can say "at 4:23, Alex said X" — and prove it
  • Entity and topic enrichment in the same pass
  • Schema contract stable across model versions
  • Ready for production in one afternoon
