The builder's problem

You need more than a transcript.
You need a usable data object.

Raw text from speech-to-text is table stakes. The real work starts after the transcript. Here's what that looks like in practice — and why it slows down every AI product team that reaches for it.

Symptom cluster 1

The RAG retrieval problem

Transcript chunks don't give you temporal context. A retrieval result says "the meeting mentioned pricing" but not at which point, by whom, or in what context. Your AI answers feel ungrounded because they are.

Symptom cluster 2

The agent grounding problem

Your agent has no structured way to cite audio/video sources. It can say "the recording mentioned X" but can't point to the exact moment. Agents built on raw transcripts hallucinate context they never had.

Symptom cluster 3

The post-processing tax

Every team we talk to has built their own Whisper pipeline: ffmpeg normalization, timestamp alignment, speaker diarization, entity extraction. That's weeks of work before you ship one useful feature.

Symptom cluster 4

The schema instability problem

OpenAI changes their Whisper output format. Your parsing logic breaks. Your downstream systems break. You're now maintaining someone else's model output contract — and it's not your core competency.

Symptom cluster 5

The real-time latency problem

Streaming audio needs streaming intelligence — partial transcripts as they happen, timestamps updating in real time, session state across chunks. Batch transcription doesn't solve real-time product features.

Symptom cluster 6

The multi-source problem

YouTube URLs, uploaded recordings, live streams, conference calls — all require different ingestion paths. Most teams end up with a fragmented pipeline that doesn't scale beyond their first use case.

Objection handling

Questions builders actually ask.

"We already run Whisper ourselves."

You do. And your team spends time maintaining the pipeline, not building features. Signal Loom is the schema contract and the processing layer — what you'd build next anyway. The pitch isn't Whisper. The pitch is what you'd do with Whisper if you didn't have to maintain it.

"We can add timestamps ourselves."

Maybe. But you also need provenance tracking, speaker labeling, entity extraction, schema stability, streaming, and multi-source ingestion. Each one is a project. Signal Loom gives you all of them in one call.

"This seems like an extra dependency."

The dependency you have is Whisper plus every post-processing step you're maintaining. Signal Loom replaces that stack. The API contract is smaller than the pipeline you're already maintaining.

"How is this different from AssemblyAI or Deepgram?"

They're speech-to-text engines. Signal Loom is a structured intelligence layer. We use best-in-class STT underneath. The product is the schema — timestamps, speakers, topics, entities, provenance — not just the transcript.
